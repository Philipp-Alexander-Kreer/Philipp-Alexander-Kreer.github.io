Sample Project: Sandbag Detection through Noise-injection

AGI Deception Detection Hackathon Winner & Accepted to NeurIPS 2024

Motivation: There is an incentive for AI developers to develop AI systems that intentionally hide risky capabilities, such as hazardous biological, chemical and cyberattack knowledge, to avoid government regulations (e.g. the European Union AI Act)

Project Target: Detection of strategic under performance - sandbagging - in advanced large language models (e.g., Llama-3.1-70B).

Method: I coded a noise-injection algorithm in PyTorch, with which I add increasing random noise to the model's parameters. The noise disrupts the deceptive behavior.

Results: Suddenly the model performs significantly better than without noise which indicates sandbagging.

Patagonia: The Land of Fire and Ice

After finishing my PhD, in September 2025, I'm planning to explore Patagonia's wilderness on the The Greater Patagonian Trail (GPT). This path network links Santiago de Chile to the Drake Passage, the southernmost tip of South America. Its remote trail offers a diverse landscape of glaciers, volcanoes, steppes, rainforests, and fjords, along with encounters with indigenous Mapuche communities, Chilean cowboys known as arrierros, and resilient hermits.

The GPT intertwines Adventure, Culture, and Nature, offering an unparalleled journey. However, its unpredictable nature demands meticulous preparation. Presently, I'm immersing myself in local Radio channels and Chilean literature to improve my Spanish skills and learn about the local culture. I'm also testing my equipment, such as GPS systems, in the alps and train myself in new skills like pack rafting.
