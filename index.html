AI Safety  ·  TU Munich & APART Research  ·  2024–2025

Noise Injection Reveals Hidden Capabilities of Sandbagging Language Models

How do you catch a lying AI? You make it drunk.

Just like humans reveal more than they intend after a few drinks, AI systems expose hidden capabilities when you shake them up during interrogation. In mathematical terms: inject random noise into all model parameters during benchmark evaluation. The deception breaks down, the true capabilities surface.

I used this to detect strategic deception in models like Phi3, Llama 3, and Mistral. First author at NeurIPS 2024 workshop, second author at NeurIPS 2025. Featured in the AI2027 report.

AI Safety  ·  Timaeus Research Inc.  ·  2025–Present

Bayesian Influence Functions for Hessian-Free Data Attribution

How does training data shape model behaviour? We traced it back to individual training samples.

Every AI journey starts with training data. Models can be poisoned through it, hijacked through backdoors, or triggered by hidden vulnerabilities.

We traced the behaviour of up to 3 billion parameter models back to individual training tokens using Bayesian influence functions. This is forensics for AI — understanding exactly which data shaped which behaviour, so we can build systems that are safe from the ground up.

First author at ICML 2025 workshop and ICLR 2026.

Theoretical Physics  ·  TU Munich  ·  PhD

Feynman Diagram Automation

What is the origin of mass? I drew thousands of cartoons to find out.

Each one describing how elementary particles collide or how black holes collide. These innocent-looking sketches are called Feynman diagrams, and they hide the terrifying and beautiful mathematics of General Relativity and Quantum Field Theory.

My PhD at TU Munich focused on automating their evaluation for Higgs boson production at CERN's Large Hadron Collider and for merging black holes.
